# 📊 Employee Exit Analysis Dashboard

A data-driven analysis of employee attrition and workplace satisfaction to identify key drivers and recommend retention strategies.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee exit survey data from DETE (Department of Education, Training, and Employment) to understand the key reasons behind employee attrition and evaluate workplace satisfaction.

The goal is to derive actionable insights that can help improve employee retention and overall workplace experience.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns  
- Identify key reasons for employee exits  
- Evaluate workplace satisfaction across multiple factors  
- Explore demographic trends (age, gender, tenure)  
- Provide data-driven recommendations for improving retention  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Power BI  
- Power Query  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Excel  

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

- Handled missing values (e.g., "N/A", Not Stated, nulls)  
- Removed incomplete or blank records  
- Standardized data types for accurate analysis  
- Unpivoted multiple columns (reasons & satisfaction factors)  
- Created structured fields such as **Reason** and **Factor**  
- Engineered new features like **Tenure** and **Tenure Groups**  

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

- Analyzed attrition trends using bar charts, treemaps, and distribution visuals  
- Identified top attrition drivers such as work-life balance  
- Observed tenure-based patterns (early exits & long-tenure exits)  
- Examined demographic trends (age & gender distribution)  
- Evaluated satisfaction levels using average scores across multiple factors  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Work-life balance is the leading driver of employee attrition  
- A large number of responses fall under "None of the above", indicating potential gaps in survey design  
- Attrition is high among both early-tenure (0–5 years) and long-tenured employees (30+ years)  
- Higher exits observed in older age groups, suggesting retirement as a key factor  
- Satisfaction is moderate overall, with lower scores in wellness, morale, and employee voice  
- Female employees show higher attrition compared to male employees  

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Introduce flexible working policies to improve work-life balance  
- Improve employee feedback systems through regular surveys  
- Strengthen onboarding and engagement for early-tenure employees  
- Implement succession planning for long-tenured and retiring employees  
- Enhance wellness programs and stress management initiatives  
- Encourage employee participation and voice in decision-making  

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features

- KPI cards (Total Employees, Avg Satisfaction, Attrition %)  
- Attrition by Type  
- Top Reasons for Leaving (Treemap)  
- Satisfaction Analysis by Factors  
- Demographic Analysis (Age, Gender, Tenure)  
- Interactive slicers for dynamic filtering  

---

## 🚀 Conclusion

This project highlights that employee attrition is influenced by both controllable factors (such as work-life balance and workplace environment) and structural factors (such as retirement). Addressing these areas can significantly improve employee retention and satisfaction.
